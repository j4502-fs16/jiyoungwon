Communications and PR specialist

When I was only 15, I flew more than 12 hours from Seoul, South Korea, to the United States to start my study abroad journey. During my first year, I lived in Oregon with a wonderful host family, and a year later, I moved to a small town in Minnesota, where I finished the three remaining years of high school.

In 2008, I moved again. This time, the move was to start my college life at the Missouri School of Journalism. During my study, I experienced hands-on journalism and learned reporting, copy editing and news design by working at the Columbian Missourian, a daily, community news organization directed by professional editors and staffed by the university's journalism students. I became a stronger person by managing to work under deadlines and meeting new people with different stories.

One of the highlights of my college experience is the eight-month internship at The Associated Press in Seoul. It felt unreal to help AP set up its bureau in Pyongyang, North Korea, which became the first international news organization with a full-time presence in the country. It felt even more unreal when I heard live on the North Korean state channel that Kim Jong Il died. I will never forget that moment when the Seoul news editor dialed the AP headquarters in New York and screamed, "Breaking news, breaking news. File the obit!."

Close to graduating from the university, I had a lot in my mind about what to do next. I enjoyed news design more than reporting and editing, and a part of me wanted to learn communications and public relations. The idea of being the bridge between organizations and the public was appealing. I also felt confident that with my wide range of journalism experiences and my personality, I was in a great position to pursue this career path.

Since the first day at ASNE, I've enjoyed every second of what I do as communications coordinator. I've improved my writing, editing and design skills, mastered new skills that come handy in the field of public relations and become a more effective communicator. I can't wait to see myself continue to grow. I look forward to my next journey when the time comes!
